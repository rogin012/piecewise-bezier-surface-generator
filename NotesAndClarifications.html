aNotes and clarifications on Biquartic C1 Spline Surfaces Over Irregular Meshes by Jorg Peters Section 2 When it says "Associated with each pair, cell and cell vertex, are two scalar weights", it means that each pair of original cell and vertex have a pair of scalar weights. After mesh refinement, that means that each sub-cell has its own pair of scalar weights. Section 2.1 - Midpoint Mesh Refinement There are two vertex types, M and P Vertices of type M are the ones on the edges of the original mesh, formed during subdivision.* They always have 4 neighbors, since in the original mesh they were an edge (with two neighbors) that was then divided in half. Vertices of type P are the original mesh's vertices, along with the centroids of each original cell in the mesh.* They can have 3+ neighbors If they were originally a vertex in the original mesh, the number of neighbors is the same as in the original mesh. If they're the centroid of an original cell, then the number of neighbors is equal to the number of sides of the original cell. If all of the original cells are already quadrilaterals, the midpoint mesh refinement step isn't needed.
